Discover the perfume that takes six years to create

1 month ago
  • Share
  • Tweet

In partnership withArizona by Proenza Schouler

Imagine that you and a friend are embarking on a once-in-a-lifetime road trip across America, taking some time out to actually talk to each other. You haven’t overthought it and don’t have a strict itinerary to stick to. You’re just going to see where the open road takes you.

You’re deep within canyon-land, but you’re still checking in with friends and family, reading the odd email and uploading pictures of your trip to Instagram. Of course you are, because being constantly connected via our phones is part of our everyday lives.

Then, suddenly, your phone stops working…This scenario is exactly what happened to Proenza Schouler founders Jack McCollough and Lazaro Hernandez in 2016. Having driven through Texas, New Mexico, Utah and Arizona, the duo found themselves cut off from the world. Instead of experiencing anxiety when their tech failed, they discovered the opposite emotion: a feeling of boundless liberation.

The art of recharging

It’s this experience – of switching off and recharging – that the duo have channelled into their debut fragrance, Arizona. “It’s really less about the state, and more about the state of mind,” Hernandez says of their new perfume.

The breathtaking desert landscape has inspired artists, writers and filmmakers for generations – and it worked its magic this time, too. “Reconnecting to things that really matter feels both extremely personal and universal,” explains McCollough. “In today’s world, this feeling of wanting to connect to something more real feels right to us – the sense of the outdoors, of unexplored nature, of a kind of universal energy.”

A precious scent

Arizona’s bottle design is inspired by the state’s distinctive landscape of rugged rocks, ravines and ridges. The fragrance, meanwhile, attempts to capture the spirit of Arizona. Its unique mineral signature, created using two accords (creamy orris and white cactus flower), has been specifically designed to recapture the designers’ experiences when they were camping in the Arizona desert. The region’s white cactus flower blossoms just once a year (the duo made eight fashion collections in the time it’s taken them to create this perfume), and its scent is perfectly captured in this fragrance. Meanwhile, orris accord (one of perfumery’s most precious and expensive elements) is derived from the root of the iris, and takes up to six years to prepare.

Merging of worlds

McCollough and Hernandez wanted to create a perfume in the same way they approach their collections. Taking influences from both the past and an imagined future, the perfumers who helped craft Arizona have the combined experience to take techniques from every era

of perfume-making. While Carlos Benaim used traditional processes, Loc Dong brought modern techniques to the table, taking inspiration from plant scents from his native Vietnam and mixing them with those of the American state.

Reconnect with nature

While that open-road, wind-in-your-hair moment may be further away than you’d like, McCollough and Hernandez admit that, actually, the scent is about much more than that. “Arizona is about the emotions a road trip like ours conjures,” says Hernandez. It’s the idea of encouraging us to disconnect from the daily chaos and reconnect with nature – a fragrance that captures the spirit of adventure, the great outdoors, time out and the ability (and desire) to switch off. Your Arizona state of mind awaits…
